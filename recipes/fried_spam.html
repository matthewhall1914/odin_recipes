Spam Musubi Recipe

Salty and sweet meat with a hint of nuttiness

This dish is simple and delicious, though it isn't the greatest for your health.

Each bite of meat will bring pure joy to your tastebuds, and you won't want to stop once you start!

Ingredients

While my preference goes towards hoisin sauce, teriyaki sauce or a simple mixture of soy sauce and sugar also work well.

  • 1 can of SPAM (lite or regular)
  • 1 cup of uncooked short grain rice
  • 3 Tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 Tspn sesame oil
  • 3 sheets of nori seaweed paper

Optional Ingredients

These are simply for taste. I enjoy a light sprinkling of sesame seeds for the extra bit of texture, though furikake will do the same thing, just with a bit more flavor.

Rice vinegar, much like in sushi, gives the rice a bit more personality and helps it stay together.

  • Sesame seeds
  • Furikake
  • Rice Vinegar

Directions

  1. Prepare the rice however you prepare rice, I recommend a rice cooker.
  2. Lay the SPAM on it's side, slice it evenly, anywhere between 8 to 14 slices is thin enough cook thoroughly, but not instantly crisp and burn.
  3. Heat up a pan to medium heat, and add your sesame oil to it. The spam has plenty of fat to cook itself in, so just a little will do.
  4. Take one slice of spam, and use it to spread the oil around the pan. When finished, leave it at the 12 o' clock position on your pan.
  5. Continue laying your spam clockwise around the pan. Allow 3 minutes to fry.
  6. Flip the spam in the order that they have been laid.
  7. While the other side fries, apply a small dot of hoisin to each piece of SPAM, and spread it using a spoon or brush.
  8. After another 3 minutes, flip the coated SPAM and repeat this process again.
  9. Cook each side for 3-5 minutes. The heat must be kept low to allow the sugars in the sauce to caramelize without burning.

Serving

When finished, garnish your rice and serve. You may choose to form the rice into the shape of a SPAM slice and wrap it with nori, though I like to simply rip a strip of nori, grab a clump of rice, and grab a SPAM. It's inelegant, but just as delicious.
